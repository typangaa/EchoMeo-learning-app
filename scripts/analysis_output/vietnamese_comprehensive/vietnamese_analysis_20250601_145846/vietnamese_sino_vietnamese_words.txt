# Sino-Vietnamese Words (Chinese Origin)
# These words may be easier for Chinese speakers to learn

lớp (Chu Nom: null) - Sino-Vietnamese loanword corresponding to Chinese 班, used for class, grade, or group in educational contexts
nửa năm (Chu Nom: null) - từ Hán-Việt kết hợp 'nửa' (tiếng Việt bản địa, nghĩa là 'nửa') và 'năm' (từ Hán-Việt, tương ứng với '年' trong tiếng Trung)

phía bắc (Chu Nom: 北) - Sino-Vietnamese term derived from Chinese 北 (běi, north) combined with 边 (biān, side/part).
bản (Chu Nom: 本) - Sino-Vietnamese loanword from Chinese 'běn' (本), meaning book, edition, or original version. Used for both physical books and literary editions.

ra (Chu Nom: xuất) - sino-vietnamese loanword from Chinese 出 (chū), meaning 'to go out' or 'to emerge', adapted into Chữ Nôm as 'xuất'
mặc (Chu Nom: chuần) - Sino-Vietnamese loanword from Chinese 'chuān' (穿), originally meaning 'to wear' or 'to put on', later extended to meanings like 'to pierce' or 'to penetrate'

sai (Chu Nom: null) - sino-vietnamese term derived from Chinese 'cuò', meaning 'mistake' or 'wrong'
đập (Chu Nom:  đả) - Sino-Vietnamese loanword from Chinese 'dǎ', representing both physical striking and abstract actions like typing or mixing

mở (Chu Nom: đả khai) - sino-vietnamese loanword from Chinese 打开 (dǎ kāi), combining 打 (dǎ, to strike) and 开 (kāi, to open), though the meaning evolved to mean 'to open' in Vietnamese

thứ (Chu Nom: null) - sino-vietnamese loanword from Chinese 第 (dì), used as ordinal prefix in Vietnamese
